Regulatory Framework

MiCA & Crypto-Assets Regulation

CASP/CY/2025/0033 | EU Framework Overview

MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation) is the EU’s comprehensive framework for regulating crypto-assets and related services. As of May 2026, it is largely in full application, representing a landmark shift toward harmonized oversight across the 27 EU member states.

1. What is MiCA? Background and Objectives

MiCA (Regulation (EU) 2023/1114) entered into force on 29 June 2023. Its primary goals include:

  • Consumer/investor protection — through transparency, disclosure, and risk warnings.
  • Market integrity and financial stability — by addressing risks like market abuse and systemic issues.
  • Legal certainty and innovation — creating a single rulebook that reduces fragmentation.
  • Level playing field — between traditional finance and crypto, while enabling EU passporting.

2. Classification of Crypto-Assets

  • Asset-Referenced Tokens (ARTs) — Stablecoins backed by a basket of assets.
  • E-Money Tokens (EMTs) — Stablecoins pegged 1:1 to a single fiat currency.
  • Other Crypto-Assets — Utility tokens, requiring a white paper for public offers.

3. CASPs: Authorization and Operating Requirements

Crypto-Asset Service Providers (CASPs) must be authorized. Services include custody, trading platforms, execution of orders, and portfolio management. LUMEN Bank's crypto services are provided under our CySEC CASP registration (No. CASP/CY/2025/0033), ensuring full compliance with MiCA regulations including:

  • Client asset segregation and protection.
  • Transparent pricing and conflict-of-interest management.
  • Continuous monitoring, reporting, and compliance with the EU’s Travel Rule (TFR).
